# Cross-Country Ski Marathons & Citizen Races: Europe (Excluding Scandinavia)

Research compiled: 2026-03-10
Scope: Norway, Sweden, Finland excluded (covered separately)

---

## Series Memberships Key

- **WL** = Worldloppet member
- **EL** = Euroloppet member
- **SC** = Ski Classics Pro Tour / Challengers
- **SL** = Swiss Loppet series
- **MST** = Marathon Ski Tour (France)
- **RL** = Russialoppet

## Italy

### 1. Marcialonga
- **Location**: Moena to Cavalese (Val di Fassa / Val di Fiemme), Trentino
- **Typical date**: Last Sunday of January
- **Distances**: 70 km (main), 45 km (Light, finishes in Predazzo)
- **Elevation gain**: ~1,030 m total; max altitude 1,450 m
- **Technique**: Classic only (reverted from freestyle in 2003)
- **Field size**: ~7,000-8,000 participants
- **Founded**: 1971
- **Description**: Italy's most famous and largest XC ski marathon, traversing two Dolomite valleys. One of the pillars of both Worldloppet and Ski Classics Pro Tour.
- **Website**: https://marcialonga.it/
- **Series**: WL, SC (Pro Tour)

### 2. Granfondo Dobbiaco-Cortina (Toblach-Cortina)
- **Location**: Dobbiaco (Toblach) to Cortina d'Ampezzo, South Tyrol / Veneto
- **Typical date**: First weekend of February
- **Distances**: 42 km classic, 35 km skating
- **Elevation gain**: ~500 m; highest point Passo Cimabanche at 1,529 m
- **Technique**: Classic (42 km) and Freestyle (35 km)
- **Field size**: ~2,500-3,000
- **Founded**: 1977
- **Description**: Historic race following the former "Ferrovia delle Dolomiti" railway line through two illuminated tunnels. UNESCO Natural World Heritage scenery.
- **Website**: https://www.dobbiacocortina.org/en/
- **Series**: WL

### 3. La Sgambeda
- **Location**: Livigno, Lombardy
- **Typical date**: Mid-December (season opener)
- **Distances**: 30 km (main), shorter options available
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate (high plateau at ~1,800 m)
- **Technique**: Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~1,500-2,000
- **Founded**: 1991 (35th edition in 2025)
- **Description**: Season-opening granfondo on Livigno's high-altitude plateau. Part of the Ski Classics Pro Tour circuit, attracting both amateurs and elite professionals.
- **Website**: https://www.lasgambeda.it/en/
- **Series**: SC (Pro Tour)

### 4. 3 Zinnen Ski-Marathon (formerly Pustertaler Ski-Marathon)
- **Location**: Sexten to Niederdorf, South Tyrol
- **Typical date**: Second weekend of January
- **Distances**: 62 km classic, 30 km classic, 30 km freestyle
- **Elevation gain**: Significant; course traverses Dolomite valleys
- **Technique**: Classic and Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~1,500-2,000
- **Founded**: 1976 (50th edition in 2026)
- **Description**: One of South Tyrol's oldest and most traditional XC ski marathons, now part of the Ski Classics Pro Tour. Scenic route through the Three Peaks Dolomites region.
- **Website**: https://www.ski-marathon.com/en/
- **Series**: SC (Pro Tour)

### 5. Gsiesertal Lauf (Gran Fondo Val Casies)
- **Location**: Val Casies (Gsieser Valley), South Tyrol
- **Typical date**: Third weekend of February (late Feb/early Mar in 2026 due to Olympics)
- **Distances**: 42 km, 30 km (both classic and freestyle), 3 km and 5 km kids
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate rolling terrain
- **Technique**: Classic and Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~2,400 participants
- **Founded**: 1984
- **Description**: Italy's second-largest ski marathon. Never cancelled due to weather since inception. Number of participants roughly matches the valley's population. Part of Ski Classics Challengers.
- **Website**: https://www.valcasies.com/en/
- **Series**: SC (Challengers), EL

### 6. Südtirol Moonlight Classic (Seiser Alm / Alpe di Siusi)
- **Location**: Compatsch, Seiser Alm, South Tyrol
- **Typical date**: Late January / early February (full moon night)
- **Distances**: 30 km, 15 km
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate (plateau at ~1,800-2,000 m)
- **Technique**: Classic only
- **Field size**: ~1,000-1,500
- **Founded**: 2009 (18th edition in 2026)
- **Description**: Unique night race on Europe's largest high-altitude plateau, illuminated by 1,000 torches and full moon. Mass start at 8 PM. A magical atmosphere event.
- **Website**: https://www.moonlightclassic.info/en/
- **Series**: SC (Challengers)

### 7. Marcia Gran Paradiso
- **Location**: Cogne, Aosta Valley
- **Typical date**: Mid-March
- **Distances**: 40 km (Challengers, sometimes shortened to 30 km), 15 km
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate; course at ~1,500 m altitude
- **Technique**: Classic (main) and Freestyle options
- **Field size**: ~800-1,200
- **Founded**: 1975 (celebrated 40th edition recently)
- **Description**: Historic race in Italy's oldest national park, starting from the Sant'Orso meadows below the Gran Paradiso massif. Part of Ski Classics Challengers.
- **Website**: https://www.marathondugrandbec.com/ (check local sites)
- **Series**: SC (Challengers)

## Switzerland

### 8. Engadin Skimarathon
- **Location**: Maloja to S-chanf, Upper Engadin Valley, Graubünden
- **Typical date**: Second Sunday of March
- **Distances**: 42 km (main), 21 km (half)
- **Elevation gain**: Net downhill (start 1,820 m, finish 1,670 m); ~200 m of climbing
- **Technique**: Freestyle (skating)
- **Field size**: ~13,500 participants from 60+ nations
- **Founded**: 1969
- **Description**: Switzerland's largest mass sports event and the world's second-largest XC ski race. A frozen-lake classic through the stunning Upper Engadin. Part of Worldloppet and Swiss Loppet.
- **Website**: https://www.engadin-skimarathon.ch/
- **Series**: WL, SL

### 9. Engadin La Diagonela
- **Location**: Pontresina to Zuoz, Upper Engadin Valley, Graubünden
- **Typical date**: Third Saturday of January
- **Distances**: 55 km (main, sometimes shortened to 47 km), plus La Pachifica (shorter)
- **Elevation gain**: Rolling terrain at 1,800+ m altitude
- **Technique**: Classic (main race); La Sfida in freestyle
- **Field size**: ~1,400 max
- **Founded**: 2014
- **Description**: The largest classic-technique XC race in Switzerland. Part of Ski Classics Pro Tour since Season IV. Stunning high-altitude Engadin landscapes.
- **Website**: https://www.engadin-skimarathon.ch/ (shared with Engadin Skimarathon)
- **Series**: SC (Pro Tour), SL

### 10. International Gommerlauf
- **Location**: Obergoms (Ulrichen), Valais
- **Typical date**: Last weekend of February
- **Distances**: 42 km marathon (skating), 21 km Gommer Classic (classic), 21 km Gommerlauf (freestyle)
- **Elevation gain**: ~400 m
- **Technique**: Freestyle (marathon) and Classic (21 km)
- **Field size**: ~1,500
- **Founded**: 1973 (54th edition in 2026)
- **Description**: One of the Alpine region's most ambitious popular races. Two laps of a 21 km course starting from the Nordic Center in Ulrichen. Part of Euroloppet and Swiss Loppet.
- **Website**: https://www.goms.ch/en/gommerlauf
- **Series**: EL, SL

### 11. Planoiras Volkslanglauf
- **Location**: Lenzerheide, Graubünden
- **Typical date**: Second Sunday of January
- **Distances**: 25 km (main), 13 km (Planoirino)
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate
- **Technique**: Freestyle (skating)
- **Field size**: ~500-800
- **Founded**: 1986 (41st edition in 2026)
- **Description**: Traditional folk XC race through the deeply snow-covered winter landscape of the Lenzerheide holiday region. Swiss Loppet series opener.
- **Website**: https://arosalenzerheide.swiss/en/Lenzerheide/Top-Events/Sports/Planoiras-Folk-Cross-Country-Skiing-Race
- **Series**: SL

### 12. Rothenthurmer Volksskilauf
- **Location**: Rothenthurm, Schwyz
- **Typical date**: Third Sunday of January

- **Elevation gain**: Moderate
- **Technique**: Freestyle (skating)

## Germany

### 18. König Ludwig Lauf
- **Location**: Oberammergau, Bavaria (Ammergauer Alps)
- **Typical date**: First or second weekend of February
- **Distances**: 50 km, 23 km (both classic and skating available)
- **Elevation gain**: ~830 m on the 50 km course; climbs from 840 m to 1,000+ m
- **Technique**: Classic and Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~4,000+ from 30+ nations
- **Founded**: 1968
- **Description**: Germany's most popular and largest XC ski race. Named after King Ludwig II, the course passes his Linderhof Palace in the Ammertal valley. Worldloppet founding member.
- **Website**: https://www.koenig-ludwig-lauf.com/
- **Series**: WL, SC (Challengers)

### 19. Skadi Loppet
- **Location**: Bodenmais-Bretterschachten, Bavarian Forest
- **Typical date**: Late January (moved from March for snow reliability)
- **Distances**: 42 km, 22 km (both classic and freestyle)
- **Elevation gain**: 1,000+ m total; altitude 1,040-1,300 m
- **Technique**: Classic (Saturday) and Freestyle (Sunday)
- **Field size**: ~1,000-1,500
- **Founded**: ~1990s (30+ years)
- **Description**: One of Central Europe's best-known ski marathons, with exceptional snow reliability at high altitude. Hosted European Ski Marathon Championships 2026. Among the most demanding courses in the ski marathon scene.
- **Website**: https://www.skadi-loppet.de/en
- **Series**: EL

### 20. Rennsteig Ski-Lauf
- **Location**: Oberhof, Thuringian Forest
- **Typical date**: Mid-February
- **Distances**: 30 km classic, 15 km classic, 15 km freestyle
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate; trails at 500-980 m altitude
- **Technique**: Classic and Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~500-1,000
- **Founded**: ~1970s
- **Description**: Winter counterpart to the famous Rennsteiglauf running race. Set along the Rennsteig ridgeway, the longest continuously signposted long-distance ski trail in Central Europe (142 km).
- **Website**: https://www.ahotu.com/event/rennsteig-ski-lauf
- **Series**: EL (partner)

### 21. Lemming Loppet
- **Location**: Northern Black Forest, Baden-Württemberg
- **Typical date**: February
- **Distances**: Various (modeled on Vasaloppet format)
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate Black Forest terrain
- **Technique**: Freestyle (skating)
- **Field size**: ~200-500
- **Founded**: 2016
- **Description**: Newer marathon modeled on Sweden's Vasaloppet. Set in the Black Forest, one of Germany's traditional XC skiing regions. Growing event.
- **Website**: https://lemming-loppet.de/en/

## Austria

### 22. Dolomitenlauf
- **Location**: Obertilliach to Lienz, East Tyrol
- **Typical date**: Late January
- **Distances**: 42 km, 20 km (both classic and freestyle); 4 km short
- **Elevation gain**: ~230 m per lap; start altitude ~1,450 m (snow-sure)
- **Technique**: Classic and Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~2,000-2,500
- **Founded**: 1970 (founding Worldloppet member)
- **Description**: Austria's biggest and most famous XC ski race. A founding Worldloppet member, the race follows the Gail river from Obertilliach's biathlon center. Guaranteed snow conditions at altitude.
- **Website**: https://www.dolomitensport.at/en/events/dolomitenlauf.html
- **Series**: WL

### 23. Ganghoferlauf
- **Location**: Leutasch, Seefeld region, Tyrol
- **Typical date**: Last weekend of February / first weekend of March
- **Distances**: 50 km classic, 25 km classic, 42 km skating, 22 km skating, 10 km fun
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate; course at ~1,100-1,300 m
- **Technique**: Classic and Freestyle (separate races)
- **Field size**: ~1,800+
- **Founded**: 1968 (59th edition in 2026)
- **Description**: Austria's largest and oldest popular XC ski race. Home of the European Ski Marathon Championships. Set in the Seefeld Olympic cross-country region.
- **Website**: https://www.seefeld.com/en/ganghoferlauf.html
- **Series**: EL, SC (Challengers)

### 24. Koasalauf
- **Location**: St. Johann in Tirol
- **Typical date**: Historically January/February (currently on hiatus)
- **Distances**: 45 km, 22 km, 7 km (when active)
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate; Wilder Kaiser mountain backdrop
- **Technique**: Classic and Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~1,000+ (historically)
- **Founded**: ~1970s
- **Description**: One of Austria's major XC citizen races, set against the Wilder Kaiser mountains. Currently on hiatus (no race in 2025/26 due to organizational challenges and volunteer shortages).
- **Website**: https://www.koasalauf.at/en/
- **Series**: EL

### 25. Bad Gastein Criterium
- **Location**: Sportgastein, Salzburg
- **Typical date**: Mid-December (season opener)
- **Distances**: 36 km
- **Elevation gain**: Significant; course at 1,600 m altitude with "The Wall" climbs
- **Technique**: Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~500-1,000 (Pro Tour event)
- **Founded**: ~2010s (Ski Classics era)
- **Description**: High-altitude Pro Tour season opener at 1,600 m in Sportgastein. Multi-lap format with tactical sprint and climbing competitions. Part of Ski Classics Pro Tour.
- **Website**: https://www.gastein.com/en/events/winter/gastein-classics/
- **Series**: SC (Pro Tour)

## France

### 26. La Transjurassienne
- **Location**: Lamoura (Jura) to Mouthe (Doubs), Jura Mountains
- **Typical date**: Second weekend of February
- **Distances**: 68 km (main, reduced from 76 km in 2015), 50 km Classic, 25 km, 20 km
- **Elevation gain**: ~1,600 m total; start at 1,166 m, finish at 945 m
- **Technique**: Freestyle (main 68 km), Classic (50 km)
- **Field size**: ~4,000 from 30 nations
- **Founded**: 1979 (first race 1980)
- **Description**: France's most iconic XC ski marathon and the country's largest. Point-to-point race through 10 Jura mountain villages. Worldloppet member since 1981.
- **Website**: https://www.latransju.com/en/
- **Series**: WL

### 27. La Foulée Blanche
- **Location**: Autrans-Méaudre en Vercors, Isère
- **Typical date**: Fourth weekend of January
- **Distances**: 42 km (main), plus shorter options down to 1.5 km
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate; Vercors plateau at ~1,000-1,200 m
- **Technique**: Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~2,000-3,000
- **Founded**: 1979 (48th edition in 2026)
- **Description**: Long-running popular event where amateurs ski alongside current and veteran champions. Set on the scenic Vercors plateau.
- **Website**: https://www.lafouleeblanche.com/
- **Series**: MST

### 28. Marathon International de Bessans
- **Location**: Bessans, Haute Maurienne Vanoise, Savoie
- **Typical date**: Second weekend of January
- **Distances**: Saturday: 30 km classic, 15 km classic; Sunday: 60 km, 42 km, 23 km freestyle, 10 km
- **Elevation gain**: Mostly flat plateau with short steep climbs; altitude ~1,750 m
- **Technique**: Classic (Saturday) and Freestyle (Sunday)
- **Field size**: ~1,000-1,500
- **Founded**: 1980
- **Description**: New Year season opener at 1,750 m altitude. Part of both Euroloppet and Marathon Ski Tour. Wide-open tracks across the Haute Maurienne Vanoise valley.
- **Website**: https://www.bessans.com/en/
- **Series**: EL, MST, SC (Challengers)

### 29. La Savoyarde
- **Location**: La Féclaz, Savoie
- **Typical date**: First weekend of February
- **Distances**: 42 km freestyle (main)
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate; Savoie mountain terrain
- **Technique**: Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~800-1,200
- **Founded**: ~1980s
- **Description**: Part of the Marathon Ski Tour circuit, held at the La Féclaz Nordic ski area. Also a Ski Classics Challengers event.
- **Website**: Check Marathon Ski Tour / FFS
- **Series**: MST, SC (Challengers)

### 30. Marathon du Grand Bec
- **Location**: Champagny-en-Vanoise (Champagny-le-Haut), Savoie
- **Typical date**: Fourth Sunday of February
- **Distances**: 42 km, 21 km, 10 km, 5 km, 3 km, 1.5 km
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate to significant; mountain plateau setting
- **Technique**: Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~600-1,000
- **Founded**: ~1980s
- **Description**: Popular race on the magnificent plateau of Champagny-le-Haut. Fifth leg of the Marathon Ski Tour. Scenic views of the Gran Paradiso massif.
- **Website**: https://www.marathondugrandbec.com/
- **Series**: MST

## Czech Republic

### 31. Jizerska 50 (Jizerska Padesatka)
- **Location**: Bedrichov, Jizera Mountains, Liberec
- **Typical date**: Late January / early February
- **Distances**: 50 km classic (main), 25 km classic, 10 km classic, 30 km freestyle, 1.5 km sprint
- **Elevation gain**: ~800-1,000 m; mountain course at 700-1,000 m altitude
- **Technique**: Classic (main) and Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~5,000-7,000 (approaching 200,000 total lifetime participants)
- **Founded**: 1968
- **Description**: Czech Republic's most famous XC ski race, originally a performance test for mountaineers. Memorial event for 15 mountaineers lost in the 1970 Peru expedition. Worldloppet member.
- **Website**: https://jiz50.cz/en/
- **Series**: WL, SC (Pro Tour)

### 32. Bedrichov Night Light Marathon
- **Location**: Bedrichov, Jizera Mountains, Liberec
- **Typical date**: Late January (evening race, preceding Jizerska 50)
- **Distances**: 30 km, 17.5 km
- **Elevation gain**: Similar to Jizerska 50 terrain
- **Technique**: Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~500-1,000
- **Founded**: ~2000s
- **Description**: Atmospheric evening race on the same trails as the Jizerska 50, illuminated by headlamps and moonlight. Traditional SkiTour season opener.
- **Website**: https://www.ski-tour.cz/en/
- **Series**: Part of CEZ SkiTour

### 33. Sumavsky Skimaraton
- **Location**: Kvilda, Sumava National Park (Bohemian Forest)
- **Typical date**: Late February / early March
- **Distances**: ~42 km, 25 km (varies by year)
- **Elevation gain**: Significant; altitude 860-1,230 m
- **Technique**: Classic and Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~500-1,000
- **Founded**: ~1990s
- **Description**: Set in the heart of the Sumava National Park near the Austrian/Bavarian border. Rated by Euroloppet runners as one of the best ski marathon events in Europe. Beautiful forest and open plateau tracks.
- **Website**: Check Euroloppet calendar
- **Series**: EL

## Poland

### 34. Bieg Piastow
- **Location**: Jakuszyce, Jizera Mountains, Lower Silesia (Szklarska Poreba)
- **Typical date**: Mid-March (9-day festival; main races in final 3 days)
- **Distances**: 50 km classic (main), 30 km freestyle, 25 km classic; plus shorter races
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate; mountain terrain at ~800-900 m
- **Technique**: Classic (main) and Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~5,000 from 25+ countries
- **Founded**: 1976
- **Description**: Poland's largest and most prestigious XC ski race. A 9-day festival with 13 races total. Worldloppet member. Cross-border terrain shared with the Czech Jizera Mountains.
- **Website**: https://bieg-piastow.pl/
- **Series**: WL

## Greenland

### 42. Arctic Circle Race
- **Location**: Sisimiut, West Greenland
- **Typical date**: Early April (3-day stage race)
- **Distances**: 160 km (3-day main), 100 km (3-day shorter)
- **Elevation gain**: Very significant; mountainous backcountry wilderness
- **Technique**: Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~150-300 (limited by wilderness format)
- **Founded**: 1998
- **Description**: Known as "the world's toughest cross-country skiing race." A 3-day adventure race through Greenland's backcountry wilderness, with participants camping in the snow for two nights. No other race combines competition with 160 km of wilderness skiing and backcountry camping.
- **Website**: https://acr.gl/
- **Series**: Independent

## Russia

> **Note**: Demino Marathon's Worldloppet membership was suspended from March 2022 due to the invasion of Ukraine. Russialoppet series continues domestically.

### 43. Demino Ski Marathon
- **Location**: Demino (20 km from Rybinsk, ~350 km from Moscow), Yaroslavl Oblast
- **Typical date**: Early March
- **Distances**: 50 km freestyle (main), 25 km classic
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate; rolling forested terrain
- **Technique**: Freestyle and Classic
- **Field size**: ~3,600+ (from 58 regions in 2021)
- **Founded**: ~2000s
- **Description**: Russia's most popular ski marathon. Member of Russialoppet since 2008. Worldloppet membership suspended since March 2022.
- **Website**: https://deminom.ru/en/
- **Series**: RL (WL suspended)

### 44. Murmansk Ski Marathon (Prazdnik Severa)
- **Location**: Murmansk, Kola Peninsula
- **Typical date**: Late March
- **Distances**: 50 km freestyle, 25 km freestyle, 50 km classic, 25 km classic
- **Elevation gain**: Moderate; starts on outskirts above Murmansk into tundra
- **Technique**: Classic and Freestyle (separate days)
- **Field size**: ~1,000-2,000
- **Founded**: 1974
- **Description**: Part of the historic Polar Olympiad "Celebration of the North" (Prazdnik Severa). Course leads into higher tundra with hilly forest sections and frozen lakes. Part of Russialoppet and formerly Euroloppet.
- **Website**: https://russialoppet.ru/en/
- **Series**: RL, EL (formerly)

### 45. Ugra Ski Marathon
- **Location**: Khanty-Mansiysk, Siberia
- **Typical date**: Late March / early April
- **Distances**: 50 km, 25 km, 5 km (freestyle)
- **Elevation gain**: Minimal; flat course in Gornaya River floodplain
- **Technique**: Freestyle
- **Field size**: ~2,000+
- **Founded**: 2013
- **Description**: Flat, mostly open course in the oil-rich Khanty-Mansiysk autonomous district. Part of Russialoppet Super Cup. Formerly Euroloppet race (2015-2017). Attracts Olympic and world champions.
- **Website**: http://ugraloppet.ru/en/
- **Series**: RL
